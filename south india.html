INFORMATION OF SOUTH INDIA PART

Over 4,000 years have passed since the beginning of recorded history in southern India, during which several dynasties and empires have come and gone. The Iron Age (c. 1200–200 BCE), the Sangam period (c. 600–300 CE), and the Medieval period (c. 1200–1550 CE) comprise the known historical periods of southern India. At different points in history, the peaks of the following dynasties were reached: Chera, Chola, Pandyan, Travancore, Cochin, Zamorin, Kolathunadu, Chalukya, Pallava, Satavahana, Rashtrakuta, Western Ganga, Kakatiya, Hoysala, Reddy dynasty, Seunas, Vijayanagara Empire, and the Bahmani Sultanate. . In reaction to the Islamic intervention, the Vijayanagara Empire arose and eventually ruled over most of southern India. It served as a barrier against the southern expansion of the Mughals and the Deccan Sultanate. The southern kingdoms, most notably Tipu Sultan's Kingdom of Mysore, resisted the new threats when European powers arrived in the 16th and 18th centuries CE, and many of them eventually fell to British conquest. The princely states that made up the remainder of southern India were administered by the British-created Madras Presidency. Southern India was linguistically split into the states of Andhra Pradesh, Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, Telangana, and Kerala following Indian independence.
